# 01 · Research Paradigm Justification

## Research Paradigm

This research adopts the **pragmatic paradigm**, which integrates technological innovation with practical problem-solving. The study seeks not only to generate scientific knowledge but also to provide an effective solution for improving municipal solid waste management through the implementation of a QR Code-based Information System supported by Artificial Intelligence.

## Research Problem

Municipal solid waste management in Amarilis District, Huánuco, presents challenges related to inefficient monitoring, limited traceability of household participation, delayed information processing, and insufficient technological support for decision-making.

## Research Motivation

The increasing demand for smart environmental management requires digital solutions capable of improving operational efficiency, citizen participation, and data-driven decision-making. Integrating QR Code technology with Artificial Intelligence provides an opportunity to modernize municipal waste management processes.

## Philosophical Position

This research follows a pragmatic perspective because knowledge is generated through the implementation, evaluation, and continuous improvement of an intelligent technological solution in a real-world context.

## Expected Scientific Contribution

The study contributes to:

- Digital transformation of environmental management.
- Intelligent monitoring of municipal solid waste.
- Integration of QR Code technology and Artificial Intelligence.
- Decision-support for local governments.
- Sustainable urban development through digital innovation.

## Practical Contribution

The proposed system is expected to improve:

- Household identification.
- Waste collection monitoring.
- Citizen participation.
- Operational efficiency.
- Municipal decision-making.

## Alignment with the Doctoral Program

This research aligns with the Doctoral Program in Deep Tech with a Focus on Artificial Intelligence and Emerging Technologies by integrating Information Systems, Artificial Intelligence, Digital Transformation, and Sustainable Environmental Management.
